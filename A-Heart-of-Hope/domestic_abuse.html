In countries all around the world, millions of women and girls are beaten by their partners, subjected to forced sex or
genital mutilation or killed for reasons of honor. During conflict, violence against women appears in many ways.
Violence against women and girls is not limited to any political or economic system in particular, but is prevalent in every
society in the world. It knows no racial boundaries, it is not cultural or economic. It is an expression of values and
historical and culturally specific rules still applied by many social and political institutions that encourage submission
and discrimination against women and girls. Women in urban areas are twice as likely as men to suffer some kind of
violence, especially in developing countries. One of the side effects of this situation is that, culturally, victims tend to be
"found guilty" of their situation no matter what tragedies they endured. The United Nations Commission notes that at
least one in three women and girls have been assaulted physically or sexually abused in her lifetime. 45% to 60% of
homicides against women occur in their own home and most are committed by their spouses. Violence is the leading
cause of death for women between 15 and 44 years, more than cancer and traffic accidents. Globally, it is estimated
that at least 1 in 3 women has been beaten, coerced into sex or suffered other abuses by some this man in their life.
Fear and shame continue to prevent many women from reporting violence and therefore the data collected is inconsistent.
Violence against women devastates lives, fractures communities, and stalls development. Women are a
vital part of our society. Every six hours the murder of a woman in Mexico occurs. In Peru according to the
Demographic and Health Survey (ENDES) 2013 INEI, 71.5% of women have endured violence from their partner.
35.7% was affected by physical violence, 8.4% by violence sexual and 67.5% for psychological or verbal violence.
Statistics of violence in the US A woman is attacked by her partner every 15 seconds. A woman is raped every six minutes.
Over 50% of women are beaten once in her life; more than a third are repeatedly beaten every year. The
evidence of the percentage of physical violence in premarital sex is 20 to 35%. 30% of female homicide victims were
killed by their husbands or boyfriends. 95% of domestic violence police reports refer to men who have beaten their
wives ... 9 out of l0 murdered women were killed by men ... 4 out of 5 murders occur at home. The main cause of death
for pregnant women in the US is murder, according to a study in Maryland. The second cause of death was a heart
attack. One in five deaths of pregnant women was a result of a violent attack, usually perpetrated by an intimate
partner. Intimate partner abuse cause more harm to the mother and her child than pregnancy related medical
problems.. ("Murder is biggest cause of death in pregnancy", March 21, 2001,
www.independent.co.uk/news/UK/Health/2001-03/death210301.shtml) Homicide is the leading cause of death for
women in the place where they work; Husbands or intimate partners commit 13,000 acts of violence against their
partners each year. (American Institute on Domestic Violence Statistics, www.aidv-usa.com/Statistics.htm.) The divorces
that occur each year, demonstrate the lack of knowledge that our people have on how to conduct their marital
relationships. Often times women are abandoned by their spouses and left to fend for themselves. These women often
do not understand the basic principles of interacting with their children and how to raise them with moral values to
achieve personal success. These women often don't have a profession or knowledge to perform a job to provide for
themselves and their children. There are different ways through which an abuser tries to control their partner, but most
are in one of four categories: Physical Violence: including hitting, slapping, pushing, kicking, squeezing, etc. and often
the abuse leads to suicide. Emotional violence: constantly criticizing, offensive nicknames, threatening, isolating the
woman from her family or friends, are all control techniques used by an abuser Economic Violence: forbidding a
woman to work, taking her money, administering her money or monitoring her expenses. Sexual Violence: An abuser
may force women to have sex or engage in practices that women do not want to demonstrate their control. No woman
should feel pressured to do anything she does not feel comfortable with. All these actions are controlled by the abuser,
leaving children traumatized by their experience with their parents. which leads to the breakdown of families. When a
woman is forced to modify her behavior for fear of the reaction of her partner, then she is being abused and controlled.
A child that comes from an abusive home, due to lack of love and attention from their parents, often turn to drugs,
alcohol, pornography, prostitution etc. Stop the cycle of abuse! It is not too late to make a change.
